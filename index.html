Putting Time into Perspective

By Mandi Cai This story was heavily inspired by WaitButWhy's piece, The Tail End.

Our perception of time changes as we age. When we're younger and experiencing the pieces of the world entirely as new stimuli, we perceive time as passing slower because everything requires adjustment. But when we age and settle into a routine — an alignment of tasks that we are familiar with to the point of autocompleting them — time passes a lot more quickly.

Some popular figures from the 1600's

The most fascinating thing to me was looking at the life spans of individuals I had learned about throughout history, and comparing them. Voltaire and Sir Isaac Newton lived longer than 80 years, whereas Pocahontas and Anne, Queen of Great Britain died on the earlier side.

Another interesting thing: the lives of these individuals overlapped. We often learn about these individuals in isolation, or as part of the curriculum for a single subject, i.e. physics for Sir Isaac Newton.
